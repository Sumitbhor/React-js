> **Definition:** Environment is the sum total of all external conditions, circumstances, and influences surrounding and affecting the growth, development, and survival of an organism. It includes all **living (biotic)** and **non-living (abiotic)** factors that interact with each other.

## 6. Stability of Ecosystem in Sustainable Environment

### ğŸ“Œ Definition of Ecosystem Stability

> Ecosystem stability refers to the **ability of an ecosystem to resist disturbances (resistance) and recover from them (resilience)** while maintaining its essential structure and functions.

### Types of Stability

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Resistance Stability** | Ability to resist disturbances and remain unchanged | Old-growth forests resist pest attacks |
| **Resilience Stability** | Ability to recover quickly after a disturbance | Grasslands recover quickly after fire |
| **Inertia** | Resistance to displacement from current state | Mature forest vs. grassland after drought |
| **Constancy** | Maintenance of variables within acceptable limits | Population size remaining relatively stable |

### Factors That Promote Ecosystem Stability

- âœ… **High Biodiversity** â€” More species â†’ more functional redundancy â†’ greater stability
- âœ… **Complex Food Webs** â€” Multiple energy pathways buffer against species loss
- âœ… **Efficient Nutrient Cycling** â€” Carbon, nitrogen, phosphorus cycles maintain productivity
- âœ… **Stable Energy Flow** â€” Consistent solar energy input sustains producers
- âœ… **Absence of Invasive Species** â€” Native species maintain established ecological relationships
- âœ… **Intact Soil Microbiome** â€” Healthy decomposers ensure nutrient cycling

### Threats to Ecosystem Stability

- âŒ Deforestation and habitat destruction
- âŒ Pollution (air, water, soil)
- âŒ Introduction of invasive alien species
- âŒ Climate change and global warming
- âŒ Overexploitation of natural resources
- âŒ Urbanization and land-use change

### Role in Sustainable Development

> **Sustainable Development** (Brundtland Commission, 1987): *"Development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs."*

A stable ecosystem is the **foundation of sustainable development**. Conservation, ecological restoration, and wise use of ecosystems are the three pillars of sustainability.
